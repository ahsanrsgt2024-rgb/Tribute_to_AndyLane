An Address of Honor

Dedicated to a Visionary Leader, Mentor, and Guardian

Dear Sir,

When I transitioned from TSD to TOD, the shift was immediate and profound. I went from a routine where I would occasionally find a reason to miss a morning meeting, to a reality where I eagerly count down the minutes to our morning briefings. Those meetings were never routine discussions; they were classrooms. Every email felt like a carefully crafted lesson sheet from a university professor. Every one-hour meeting felt like an advanced course. Time simply dissolved because your presence was so engaging, so educational, and so deeply inspiring. Every interaction carried a lesson that challenged us to think deeper, work smarter, and become better professionals.

You taught us that decisions should not be driven by assumptions but by data. You taught us that when facing an unknown problem, we must not panic or hide behind excuses; we must investigate, understand, and solve the root cause. You showed us that true improvement comes from fixing systems, not merely applying temporary solutions to symptoms.

We lived this philosophy together in the most profound way. I remember August 2025, when our container terminal was gripped by complete chaos. We could not handle the containers properly, yard pressure peaked, protests erupted, and newspapers were relentlessly publishing derogatory news about our operations. It felt like an impossible crisis. Yet, look at us today, just nine months later. Under your brilliant guidance, we now handle three times more volume so smoothly, so efficiently, that outsiders cannot even fathom how 30k TEUs are being moved without a single issue. You took a broken process, applied your vision, and engineered a miracle. All credit for this monumental turnaround goes to you.

You taught us to question the status quo when necessary, to hold people accountable without losing our humanity, to know when to stand firm and when to remain flexible. You demonstrated that what many consider impossible often becomes possible when approached with discipline, logic, persistence, and courage.

But beyond all your technical excellence, what made you extraordinary was your humanity.

You listened.
You genuinely listened to your team.

You understood where your people struggled, and before we even finished explaining our difficulties, you were already there to help us succeed. You protected your team. You empowered your team. You stood beside your people when others ignored them.

I still remember moments when external stakeholders overlooked our concerns. The moment you stepped in, everything changed. Suddenly, people listened. Suddenly, actions happened. Not because of your authority alone, but because people respected your integrity, your competence, and your unwavering commitment to doing what was right.

You never made us feel small.
You made us feel capable.
You made us feel that our voices mattered.
You made us believe that we could achieve more than we thought possible.

Throughout my life, I have spent eight years working both at home and abroad, and I have sat in the lecture halls of four famous European universities earning my Master’s degrees. Yet, the education I received under your leadership in just four months exceeds a lifetime of academic and professional learning.

That is not a reflection of time.
That is a reflection of impact.

Some leaders manage people.
Some leaders direct people.
Very few leaders transform people.

You belong to the last category.

The rules, principles, and values you taught us will remain with me throughout my career. They will become my professional compass. Long after projects are forgotten, reports are archived, and meetings are over, your lessons will continue guiding my decisions.

For me, you were never just a boss. You were a mentor. You were a teacher. You were a guardian. You were the kind of leader who inspires people to walk the extra mile, not because they are asked to, but because they genuinely want to.

As you leave, it feels as though a chapter of our lives is ending. The office will still be here. The meetings will continue. The emails will arrive. The work will go on. But something invaluable will be missing—your presence, your wisdom, your guidance, and your unwavering belief in your people.

My heart truly aches at your departure.

Yet alongside that sadness is immense gratitude. Gratitude that I had the privilege of working under your leadership, even if only for four short months. Gratitude that our paths crossed. Gratitude for every lesson, every challenge, every conversation, and every opportunity to learn from you.

Thank you for believing in us. Thank you for teaching us. Thank you for leading by example. And thank you for showing me the kind of leader I hope to become one day.

On behalf of everyone whose lives and careers you have touched, I wish you good health, happiness, and continued success in all that lies ahead. I pray for a bright future for your family, for your son, and for every dream you still wish to achieve.

You may be leaving this organization, but the mark you have left on us will remain forever.

Some people leave a position.
Some people leave an organization.
But great leaders leave a legacy.

And your legacy will live on in every one of us.

Thank you, Sir. You will always be remembered. 🥹

With the utmost respect, gratitude, and a heavy heart,
Ahsan Jawad
Assistant Manager, Terminal Operations (Documentation & Analytics)
Red Sea Gateway Terminal, Bangladesh
